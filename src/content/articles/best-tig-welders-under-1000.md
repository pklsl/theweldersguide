# Best TIG Welders Under $1000: AC/DC Reviews for 2026

TIG welding is the most precise and versatile welding process, but getting into it traditionally meant spending $1,500 or more for a quality machine. That barrier has fallen dramatically — today's sub-$1,000 market includes AC/DC inverter TIG welders with high-frequency start, foot pedal control, and legitimate aluminum welding capability.

This guide reviews the best TIG welders under $1,000 for home shops, hobbyists, and entry-level professionals.

## AC/DC vs. DC-Only TIG Welders

Before choosing a machine, decide whether you need AC capability.

**DC-only TIG welders** weld steel, stainless steel, titanium, copper, and most non-aluminum metals. They are less expensive, simpler, and fully capable for most work.

**AC/DC TIG welders** add aluminum welding. The AC output breaks up aluminum's oxide layer (melting point 3,762°F) while the metal itself melts at 1,220°F — without AC, you cannot weld aluminum by TIG. If you ever plan to weld aluminum, pay for AC/DC upfront.

## What to Look for in a TIG Welder Under $1,000

- **AC/DC capability** for aluminum (if needed)
- **High-frequency (HF) start** — initiates arc without touching tungsten to base metal
- **Foot pedal compatibility** — essential for proper amperage control
- **Adjustable AC frequency** — affects bead width and oxide cleaning on aluminum
- **Adjustable AC balance** — controls how much cleaning vs. penetration on aluminum
- **Pulse function** — reduces heat input on thin material
- **Output range** — 5–200 amps covers most work up to 3/8-inch steel
