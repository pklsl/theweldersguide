# Best Welding Gloves for Every Application: TIG, MIG, and Stick

No welding tool is more personal than your gloves. The right pair gives you heat protection, spatter resistance, and the dexterity to control your work. The wrong pair turns a precise TIG bead into a struggle, or leaves your hands burned from stick welding.

The key insight: you need different gloves for different processes. A single pair cannot optimize for both TIG dexterity and stick welding heat protection.

## Why Welding Gloves Are Not One-Size-Fits-All

Each welding process has different demands:

- **TIG welding** — Requires extreme finger sensitivity to feed filler rod precisely and feel the tungsten position. Heavy gloves kill TIG technique.
- **MIG welding** — Moderate spatter, moderate heat. Needs decent dexterity for gun manipulation with spatter protection.
- **Stick welding** — High spatter, high heat near the stinger. Needs maximum protection, dexterity is secondary.
- **Plasma cutting** — High heat, significant dross spatter. Needs durable palm protection.
- **Grinding** — Needs cut and abrasion resistance, not necessarily heat resistance.

## Glove Sizing and Fit

Gloves that are too large reduce dexterity and increase hand fatigue. Gloves that are too small cut circulation and cause heat buildup.

**Measure your hand:** Wrap a measuring tape around the widest part of your palm (excluding thumb). Use this measurement to select glove size:

| Palm Circumference | Glove Size |
|---|---|
| Under 7 inches | Small |
| 7–8 inches | Medium |
| 8–9 inches | Large |
| 9–10 inches | X-Large |
| Over 10 inches | XX-Large |

Most professional welders prefer gloves that are slightly looser when new — leather gloves conform to hand shape after break-in.

## How to Care for Welding Gloves

Good welding gloves are not cheap. Proper care extends their life significantly:

- **Never machine wash leather gloves.** Hot water and detergent destroy the leather's oils and grain.
- **Air dry away from heat sources.** Drying gloves on a heater or near flames causes the leather to stiffen and crack.
- **Condition leather periodically.** Use [Pecard Leather Dressing](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Pecard%20Leather%20Dressing&tag=theweldersguide-20) or neatsfoot oil on dry leather to restore flexibility.
- **Replace when palm is thin.** A worn palm is a burn waiting to happen. Replace gloves before the leather wears through.
- **Keep one pair per process.** Using TIG gloves for MIG quickly saturates them with spatter and stiffens the leather.
